My latest project - coming to life.

"Shattered Simplicity"


“Shattered Simplicity” demonstrates the commercialization of culture through consumerism and the postmodern ideology that art has been removed from its authenticity and, therefore, its artist. This aligns with Roland Barthes' essay "The Death of the Author" (1967), which argues that the creator's identity and intentions are irrelevant to understanding the work. In a culture that praises the plain, ceramics are no longer a form of unique expression but rather silhouettes indistinguishable from one another.

This piece provokes a multifaceted approach to postmodernism. In a museum-like frame, you may look through the lens and observe a piece of art irrelevant to its creator – a pot simply part of a mass-produced collection or a pastiche of traditional pottery styles. However, with perspective, the relationship between art and the artist is rekindled and rediscovered in its shattered, severed state.

Through this piece I aim to explore and challenge the postmodern notion that art and artist can be entirely detached, highlighting the enduring connection between creator and creation.
